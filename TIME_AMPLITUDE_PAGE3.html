What is a Probe?

Definition

A probe is an initial institutional market movement in the first 2 minutes (10:00-10:02 AM) that tests market direction and liquidity. The SIZE of this probe predicts the SIZE of the eventual reversal that follows.

Simple Terms: Institutional traders push the market down or up by 25-100+ points in the first few seconds/minutes. This "push" (probe) gauges resistance. The BIGGER the push, the BIGGER the reversal they intend to execute.

Examples:

  • Small probe (18 pts): Market at 25628. Probe DOWN to 25610. Result: ~140 pts reversal expected. ‚Üê Nov 11
  • Medium probe (73 pts): Market at 21371. Probe DOWN to 21298. Result: ~150-200 pts reversal. ‚Üê Jan 2

  • Extreme probe (1,197 pts): Market at 18405. Probe DOWN to 17208 (April anomaly). Result: ~1,200 pts reversal. ‚Üê Apr 7
